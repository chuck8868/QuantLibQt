00049 /*  PPMT : Primitive Polynomials Modulo Two
00050  *
00051  *
00052  * The encoding is as follows:
00053  *
00054  * The coefficients of each primitive polynomial are the bits of the given
00055  * integer. The leading and trailing coefficients, which are 1 for all of the
00056  * polynomials, have been omitted.
00057  *
00058  * Example: The polynomial
00059  *
00060  *      4    2
00061  *     x  + x  + 1
00062  *
00063  * is encoded as  2  in the array of polynomials of degree 4 because the
00064  * binary sequence of coefficients
00065  *
00066  *   10101
00067  *
00068  * becomes
00069  *
00070  *    0101
00071  *
00072  * after stripping off the top bit, and this is converted to
00073  *
00074  *     010
00075  *
00076  * by right-shifting and losing the rightmost bit. Similarly, we have
00077  *
00078  *   5    4    2
00079  *  x  + x  + x  + x + 1
00080  *
00081  * encoded as  13  [ (1)1101(1) ]  in the array for degree 5.
00082  */
00083
